Our Projects

Flagship research initiatives funded by the European Union and national programs

Horizon Europe

EU-ALMPO: Active Labor Market Policy Observatory

Funding Program Horizon Europe
Duration 2023-2026
Status Active

The EU-ALMPO project establishes a comprehensive digital observatory for analyzing and developing active labor market policies across European nations. This initiative combines advanced data analytics, machine learning, and policy research to create evidence-based recommendations for employment services.

Objectives:

  • Create a unified data platform integrating labor market information from multiple EU countries
  • Develop predictive models for assessing policy effectiveness and impact
  • Build interactive visualization tools for policymakers and researchers
  • Establish best practices for active labor market policy implementation

Key Innovations: Machine learning algorithms for policy impact assessment, real-time labor market trend analysis, and automated policy recommendation systems.

Digital Innovation

AGORA: Digital Talent Discovery Ecosystem

Type National Initiative
Duration 2022-2025
Status Active

AGORA is a comprehensive digital ecosystem designed to combat brain drain by connecting talented individuals with opportunities in their home countries. The platform uses intelligent matching algorithms to pair job seekers with employers, educational programs, and entrepreneurial resources.

Core Features:

  • AI-powered job matching and skills assessment
  • Digital portfolio and credentialing system
  • Career development and mentorship platform
  • Labor market intelligence dashboard
  • Integration with educational institutions and employers

Impact: AGORA aims to reverse brain drain trends by creating transparent, efficient pathways for talent to contribute to local economies while maintaining global competitiveness.

Machine Learning

For4Opt: ML-Driven Algorithm Optimization

Type Research Project
Duration 2023-2024
Status Completed

The For4Opt project develops machine learning frameworks for automated algorithm optimization. By analyzing code patterns, execution profiles, and performance metrics, the system suggests optimizations that improve computational efficiency without manual intervention.

Research Contributions:

  • Neural architecture search for algorithm design
  • Automated code profiling and bottleneck detection
  • Reinforcement learning for parameter tuning
  • Performance prediction models for algorithm selection

Applications: Scientific computing, data analytics pipelines, and real-time system optimization.

EU Initiative

Growth4Blue: Sustainable Blue Economy Development

Funding Program EU Blue Growth
Duration 2022-2025
Status Active

Growth4Blue promotes sustainable development in marine and maritime sectors through data-driven insights and collaborative research networks. The project integrates environmental monitoring data, economic indicators, and social impact metrics to guide blue economy initiatives.

Research Areas:

  • Marine data analytics and ocean health monitoring
  • Sustainable aquaculture optimization
  • Coastal tourism impact assessment
  • Maritime logistics and supply chain efficiency
  • Blue technology innovation tracking

Collaboration: Multi-stakeholder platform connecting researchers, industry partners, policymakers, and coastal communities across European regions.

Erasmus+

Microidea: Digital Skills for Entrepreneurs

Funding Program Erasmus+
Duration 2021-2024
Status Completed

Microidea develops educational programs and digital tools to enhance entrepreneurial skills in the digital economy. The project creates open educational resources, e-learning platforms, and assessment tools for aspiring entrepreneurs across Europe.

Deliverables:

  • Digital entrepreneurship curriculum and learning modules
  • Interactive business simulation platform
  • Skills assessment and certification system
  • Mentorship network connecting entrepreneurs and experts

Reach: Over 5,000 learners across 12 European countries have participated in Microidea training programs.

Public Health

Project 19-20: COVID-19 Impact Analysis

Type Emergency Response
Duration 2020-2022
Status Completed

In response to the COVID-19 pandemic, the DM Lab initiated Project 19-20 to analyze the virus's societal impact using big data analytics. The project combined epidemiological data, economic indicators, and social metrics to provide insights for policy response.

Key Analyses:

  • Real-time pandemic spread modeling and prediction
  • Economic impact assessment on labor markets
  • Social distancing measure effectiveness evaluation
  • Healthcare system capacity monitoring
  • Vulnerable population identification

Outcomes: Research findings informed public health decisions and contributed to multiple peer-reviewed publications on pandemic data science.
